CCL

Center for Classical Lexicography (CCL)

Chongqing University

The Center for Classical Lexicography (CCL) at Chongqing University, established in 2019, is China's first specialized research institution dedicated to classical dictionary compilation. It serves as a university-level research platform, bringing together top experts in classical lexicography and philology from both China and abroad.

Building upon the profound scholarly traditions of the Sichuan-Chongqing region—including the pioneering work of Yang Xiong's Fangyan (Dialects) from the Western Han and Guo Pu's Erya Zhu (Annotations on the Erya) from the Eastern Jin—the center continues this legacy of linguistic scholarship.

With the National Social Science Foundation's major project "Compilation of the Classical Latin-Chinese Dictionary" as its engine, CCL actively develops two main research directions: "Chinese Classical Lexicography" and "Western Classical Lexicography." It is currently compiling groundbreaking dictionaries such as the Homer Lexicon, Plato Dictionary, Aristotle Dictionary, and Cicero Dictionary, aiming to fill academic gaps both domestically and internationally.

Key Research Directions

• Compilation of Classical Latin-Chinese and Ancient Greek-Chinese Dictionaries

• Specialized Author Lexicons (Homer, Plato, Aristotle, Cicero)

• Digital Humanities and Computational Lexicography

• Historical and Comparative Philology

BTN

Bibliotheca Translatio Nona (BTN)

The "Nine Translations" Library

The name "Bibliotheca Translatio Nona" draws inspiration from the term "Nine Translations" (九译), which historically refers to "distant lands reached through multiple translations" and to official translators in the Han dynasty. Following the scholarly tradition of the Sichuan-Chongqing region exemplified by the "Six Translations Sage" Liao Ping and his Liuyi Guan Congshu (Six Translations Library Collection), BTN continues this legacy of transformative scholarship.

BTN is dedicated to the translation and publication of ancient Greek and Latin literature. It focuses on two main tasks: translating classical works that have not yet been rendered into Chinese, and meticulously retranslating existing Chinese translations that require refinement. The core belief is that the foundational texts of Western civilization deserve multiple, reliable Chinese translations.

As the Tang Emperor Taizong stated, "Taking history as a mirror, one can understand the rise and fall of dynasties." In this spirit, BTN aims to provide the Chinese-speaking world with a clear "mirror" to ancient Greece and Rome, facilitating a deeper understanding of Western intellectual origins and enabling meaningful cultural dialogue.

Publication Focus

• First Chinese translations of key untranslated Greek and Latin texts

• Critical retranslations of existing Chinese editions

• Scholarly bilingual editions with commentary

• Collaborative projects with international classical publishers
